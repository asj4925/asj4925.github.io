Curriculum

Math Alive is based on the US Common Core (CCSS) achievement standards. In addition,
by introducing the quantile measure of MetaMetrics®, an internationally renowned research institute,
each grade level of the program has been officially certified and meets all requirements of the Quantile measure. Based on the Quantile measure for each grade, teachers can determine a student's mathematical level and ensure they are provided customized learning services.

Aligned to the Quantile Framework

After a long period of collaboration and research, the Math Alive and MetaMetrics teams developed the Quantile measures tailored to each mathematical concept in the mathematics curriculum. Based on the mathematical learning concepts as defined in the Quantile Measure, teachers can set individual goals for students to grow, while providing high-quality, personalized mathematics education.

  • Data based on a sample of 3 million students administered
    tests that reported Quantile measures from 2020 to 2019
  • Math Alive's Content

The Quantile measure of Math Alive's covers most of the range of the Quantile measure provided by MetaMetrics in the middle of the school year by grade, and is similar in structure. Accordingly, it has been determined that the mathematical contents in Math Alive meet global standards.

Class Structure

• Math Alive reorganizes primary math concepts into 519 Concepts.

• Two to three concepts are grouped into meaningful learning units and defined as Topics

• Three to four topics are grouped into meaningful learning and assessment units and defined as Modules.
